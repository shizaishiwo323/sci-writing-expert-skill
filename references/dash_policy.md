# Dash and Compound Policy

## Goal

Distinguish standard technical compounds from AI-like invented labels.

## Default rule

Do not introduce a new hyphenated, en-dash, or em-dash expression unless it is:

- an established technical term;
- a required compound modifier;
- a numerical range;
- a defined manuscript term;
- required by journal style.

A hard ban on all hyphens is incorrect; a hard ban on decorative invention is useful.

## Usually retain

Examples, subject to field context:

- `pore-scale`;
- `time-resolved`;
- `non-invasive`;
- `first-order`;
- `two-dimensional`;
- `low-field NMR`;
- `pore-size distribution`;
- `solid-fluid interface`;
- `advection-diffusion`;
- numerical ranges such as `0-4 h`.

## Usually rewrite

Question expressions such as:

- `structure-signal framework`;
- `connectivity-breakthrough nexus`;
- `mechanism-aware interpretation`;
- `signal-mechanism bridge`;
- multi-part rhetorical labels.

Prefer ordinary syntax:

- `a framework linking structure to the measured signal`;
- `the relation between connectivity and breakthrough`;
- `interpretation based on the proposed mechanism`.

## Em dashes

Avoid using em dashes for dramatic emphasis in formal paper prose. Prefer commas, parentheses, a colon, or a separate sentence where clearer.

## Output gate

Scan for:

- newly created `word-word` expressions;
- three-part compounds;
- new terms ending in `-aware`, `-informed`, `-driven`, `-sensitive`, or `-linked`;
- em-dash interruptions.

Retain only when scientifically standard, defined, or necessary.
