# Functional Language Bank

Use these as adaptable patterns, not automatic text. Match tense, subject, evidence strength, and journal style.

## Context

- `X is widely used to...`
- `Recent work has focused on...`
- `A central challenge in X is...`
- `Understanding X is necessary for...`
- `X remains difficult to quantify under...`

## Prior research

- `Smith et al. reported that...`
- `Previous studies have shown that...`
- `Several approaches have been developed to...`
- `Method A has been applied to...`
- `Results across these studies differ with respect to...`
- `This interpretation is supported by...`

## Gap

- `However, it remains unclear whether...`
- `The effect of X under Y conditions has not been established.`
- `Existing methods are limited by...`
- `Few studies have directly compared...`
- `The mechanism responsible for X remains unresolved.`
- `These findings leave open the question of...`

Use gap claims only when literature coverage supports them.

## Present study

- `Here, we investigate...`
- `This study evaluates...`
- `We test the hypothesis that...`
- `To determine whether X, we...`
- `The present analysis compares...`
- `We develop and evaluate...`

## Methods

- `To quantify X, we measured...`
- `Samples were collected from...`
- `X was selected because...`
- `The procedure followed Y, with two modifications.`
- `Calibration was performed before each...`
- `To minimize X, we...`
- `Uncertainty was estimated using...`

## Directing to evidence

- `Figure 2 shows...`
- `As shown in Figure 2, ...`
- `The corresponding values are summarized in Table 1.`
- `The distribution shifted toward...`
- `Across all conditions, ...`
- `The largest difference occurred at...`

## Comparison

- `X was higher than Y under...`
- `The two methods produced similar estimates of...`
- `In contrast to X, Y...`
- `This pattern was consistent across...`
- `The observed values were within...`
- `The discrepancy may reflect differences in...`

## Uncertainty or problems

- `Variation among replicates was...`
- `The estimate was sensitive to...`
- `No clear trend was observed for...`
- `The model underestimated X at...`
- `This deviation did not alter...`
- `The result should be interpreted within...`
- `Because X was not measured, we cannot determine...`

## Interpretation

- `These results indicate that...`
- `The findings suggest that...`
- `This pattern is consistent with...`
- `One possible explanation is...`
- `X may contribute to...`
- `The evidence does not distinguish between...`
- `Taken together, the results support...`

## Relation to literature

- `This finding agrees with...`
- `The result extends previous work by...`
- `Unlike earlier studies, the present analysis...`
- `The apparent disagreement may arise from...`
- `These data refine the proposed relationship between...`
- `The result provides independent support for...`

## Contribution and implication

- `This study provides...`
- `The main contribution is...`
- `The method enables...` only when capability is demonstrated.
- `The findings clarify...`
- `The result may inform...`
- `Within the tested range, the model can...`

Name the contribution; do not rely on promotional adjectives.

## Limitations and future work

- `This study is limited by...`
- `Because the analysis was restricted to X, the findings may not extend to Y.`
- `The present design cannot determine whether...`
- `Future work should test...`
- `Additional measurements are needed to distinguish...`
- `Validation at X scale would determine...`

## Connectors by logic

- cause: `because`, `owing to`, `as a result of`;
- result: `therefore`, `consequently`, `thus`;
- contrast: `however`, `whereas`, `in contrast`;
- concession: `although`, `nevertheless`, `despite`;
- addition: `in addition`, `furthermore`, `also`;
- example: `for example`, `specifically`;
- sequence: `initially`, `subsequently`, `after`, `during`;
- reformulation: `that is`, `in other words`.

Do not rotate connectors merely for variety.
