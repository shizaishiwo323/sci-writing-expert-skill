# Source Notes and Transformation Policy

## Primary pedagogical source

Hilary Glasman-Deal, *Science Research Writing for Non-Native Speakers of English*, Imperial College Press, 2010.

The book teaches writers to examine representative research articles, identify conventional section structures, grammar, and functional vocabulary, and apply those patterns to their own work.

## High-level concepts adapted

This Skill independently restates and operationalizes general concepts including:

- narrowing Introductions and widening Discussions;
- functional move models for Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion/Conclusion, Abstract, and Title;
- tense as a signal of rhetorical function;
- active/passive choices and agency;
- sentence connection and paragraph organization;
- articles, modifiers, sequence, frequency, quantity, causality, and modality;
- use of representative target articles to adapt generic conventions.

## Original additions

- Codex-specific routing and progressive reference loading;
- Chinese-to-English scientific translation workflow;
- protected-content and evidence-strength protocol;
- manuscript-level promise-payoff and evidence-chain audit;
- target-journal profile extraction;
- explicit output contracts;
- integrity and copyright validation;
- trigger and behavior evaluation cases.

## Copyright policy

Do not add the source PDF, extracted book text, screenshots, exercises, answer keys, or extended examples to this repository.

When expanding the Skill:

1. extract the underlying general principle;
2. rewrite it independently;
3. add an operational decision rule or original example;
4. cite the bibliographic source here when appropriate;
5. verify that no copyrighted source file is committed.
